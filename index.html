About Me

When I left the UK to study in Canada I knew I wanted to maintain an interdisciplinary approach to my studies. As such, I spent my four years in Canada cultivating an understanding of both Philosophy and Biology. Only after a few years of this did it become clear to me that the two fields were not disjointed and distinct, instead they were attempting to provide an understanding of ourselves and our place in the world around us, and give prescriptions about what we should do given this knowledge. It is this point where the two meet that I find most interesting. In my studies I have cultivated skills in concise and clear scientific writing, developing original theses, coding in HTML and R (self-taught), First Order and Mathematical Logic, and I have been exposed to an array of different literature (both scientific, philosophical and otherwise). My interests outside of my studies include film photography, reading and writing, hiking, skiing, an interest in wildlife that took me to Costa Rica for 2 weeks following my graduation, cultivating a variety of houseplants (I have successfully grown tomatoes and avocados indoors), and an avid interest in cooking.

An Evalutation of the Modularity of Leg Striping in Horses

In my independent studies with Professor Abouheif at McGill we were assessing the possibility of leg striping as an atavism, or ancestral potential. It became clear that a necessary aspect to understand was the way striping emerges and whether there are regional biases across and within legs. To assess this we counted the number of stripes and assessed their location using ImageJ. With this coordinate Data I constructed heat maps to demonstrate the pattern. The paper is incomplete but the other undergraduate researcher and I are working to finish and publish the paper if possible.
